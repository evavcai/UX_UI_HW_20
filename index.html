Memorial Assistance Ministries

Goal

Redesign the homepage and the volunteer sign up process of Memorial Assistance Ministries’ website in an effort to better attract potential volunteers and donors, and reduce the time it takes for clients to receive assistance.

Role

User Research

User Flow

Prototyping

Timeline

3 weeks

Tools

Figma

Miro

Research

For the first phase of the project, we conducted three different types of interviews with different audiences.

Conducted 1 - 1 interviews with Director of Development & Communications & Volunteer Coordinator to better understand the structure of the organization and identify the underlying problems they face.

Conducted 1 - 1 interviews with potential MAM volunteers to understand the underlying motivations for becoming a volunteer.

Conducted usability tests on MAM’s current homepage and volunteer flow to identify frustrations and pain points.

Problem Statement

Memorial Assistance Ministries has seen a decrease in participation across three users of the organization – clients, donors, and volunteers. MAM needs greater participation from volunteers in order to better attract donors and provide more resources to the clients.

Key Insights

Through our interviews, we learned several important details about the organization that helped us with the redesign.

Most clients do not have the resources or the technical ability to open MAM’s website on a phone or desktop

75% of MAM’s clients are monoligual Spanish speakers

Potential volunteers are motivated by a community with strong values to dedicate their time to a cause

Volunteers are quite busy during the day, so efficiency and simplicity are main factors that determine whether they want to volunteer for a particular organization or not.

User Persona

With the insights from our interviews, we determined our user to be Hailey Lawson. She is impatient, doesn't like to read a lot of text, and likes websites that display relevant information in a manner that is efficient and organized. She needs to find an organization with a quick application process.

Ideation and Features

For the next phase, we conducted a team brainstorming session to ideate on features that Hailey needs in order to have a better experience signing up to volunteer at MAM. We ultimately decided on three key features that would help Hailey, the clients, and the donors better utilize the services at MAM.

Call-to-action buttons on the homepage and volunteer landing page
A volunteer application form that is integrated into MAM’s website
Switching between multiple languages

Hailey is bothered by a long application process, creating shortcuts will save her time and encourage her to sign up.

Hailey likes efficiency, creating a simpler and quicker application will enhance her experience.

75% of MAM clients are monolingual Spanish speakers and MAM is expecting an influx of Ukrainian refugees.

Usability Testing and Iterations

After completing the mid-high fidelity prototype, we conducted usability tests in order to identify any frustrations the user would have when signing up to become a volunteer. We also presented our high fidelity prototype to MAM's stakeholders, where we were commended on how inclusive the design felt for all users - volunteers, clients, and donors.

Outcomes from usability tests

Some users did not notice the call-to-action button on the homepage

Some users did not know what MAM stood for

Outcomes from stakeholder presentation

Stakeholders thought the color scheme did not represent their brand

Spelled out MAM's full name on the homepage

Filled in the CTA button to be more noticeable

Created another version of the prototype with a different color scheme

Final Prototype

Future Steps

Build out the flows for the other types of volunteers - Youth Volunteers, Group Volunteers, and Court Appointed Members

Implement a color scheme that satisfies the stakeholders of MAM

Conduct additional usability tests and continue iterating

Final Thoughts

Designing for the user and the stakeholder at the same time presents a big challenge because you have to fulfill what the stakeholders want and simultaneously deliver what the users need. Sometimes there is friction between these two parties and you must create a solution that can benefit both.
